About Me

I was born in Seoul and raised in Hong Kong. I moved to America in 2017 to study Physics at USC. However, due to my love for problem solving and my affinity for numbers, I pivoted to a major in Applied Mathematics. During my summer internship in 2019, I randomly came across some SAS guidebooks. I was fascinated by the ability to generate powerful insights from a few lines of code. This sparked my interest in analytics, and I later added a second major in Data Science. Since then, I have continually pursued analytics by learning visualization tools, database management software, and new programming languages. I hope to extend this excitement towards learning about new industries and career opportunities.

My previous internships best describe my diverse skill sets and industry experiences. My first internship was at sQoolink, an education startup in Hong Kong, where I learned the basics of growth hacking. It was exciting working alongside the founder, attending networking events, as well as familiarizing myself with exciting tools such as Mailchimp, Canva, and Excel. The following year, I worked as Research Assistant at Resolution Economics, a litigation consulting firm. During this time, I was first exposed to the corporate work environment and the management hierarchy within the company. I learned an invaluable lesson about communication with managers and maintaining a strong work ethic. My most recent internship was at Haystack Search, a brick and mortar search engine company. My day-to-day involved using SQL to query and update large amounts of product data. Overall, my internship experience outlines a wide range of roles and producing my best work to employers.

Outside of school and work, I enjoy staying active and spending quality time with friends. Powerlifting is a hobby that has stuck with me for years and I rigorously track my weights to gain a sense of progression. I like to explore LA through two avenues: hiking and food. I am also a huge fan of Formula 1 and the NBA.
